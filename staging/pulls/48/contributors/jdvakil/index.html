About Me

I am a second year PhD student at the University of Colorado Boulder advised by Prof. Alessandro Roncone. My research focuses on embodied AI, specifically in the context of robot learning. I am particularly interested in developing learning-based approaches which go beyond the realm of vision and operate in low data regimes.

In my past life, I was a researcher at Meta AI (Fundamental AI Research) where I worked on projects like RoboAgent, RT-X or Open X-embodiment, and OK-Robot.

These days, I'm working on enabling robots to learn from distributed whole-body sensing, essentially helping robots perceive and understand their peripersonal space using distributed egocentric perception data from whole-body sensors.
